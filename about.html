About

Dmitry Gofman

Mechanical engineer, multidisciplinary systems thinker, builder, and practitioner of classical yogic tools.

Dmitry GofmanFounder · Gofman Labs

I am a mechanical engineer and builder exploring the relationship between technology and human development. My work combines engineering, design, cognitive training, yogic practice, physical devices, software systems, and experimental protocols.

I am interested in technologies that do not simply make life more comfortable, but make the human being more capable, conscious, perceptive, and alive.

A multidisciplinary practice

My background is in mechanical engineering and systems thinking — the discipline of understanding how parts interact to produce behavior. Over time I became convinced that the same rigor we apply to machines can be applied to the faculties of attention, breath, memory, and perception.

Gofman Labs is where these threads meet. It is a place to prototype, measure, and refine tools at the intersection of hardware, software, and contemplative practice.

How I work

Each project begins as a question rather than a product. What would it mean to make breathing visible? Could a memory game strengthen mental imagery? Can value be tied to silence instead of computation? I build small, observe carefully, and let the experiment teach the next step.

Engineering and contemplation are not opposites. Both are disciplines of attention.

The tone of this work is direct and serious — not mystical. I believe these ideas deserve the same standards of design, evidence, and craftsmanship as any serious technology.
